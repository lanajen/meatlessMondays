Stir-Fried Broccoli and Shiitake Mushrooms

A deliciously simple and healthy main dish to go with rice.

What you'll need:

  • 2 tablespoons peanut, or neutral oil, like grapeseed or corn
  • About 1.5 pounds broccoli, trimmed, the stems cut into pieces 1/8 inch thick
  • Salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 cup vegetable stock or water
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup dried black (shiitake) mushrooms
  • 1 cup very hot water to soak mushrooms in
  • 1 teaspoon minced ginger
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  • hot pepper flakes to garnish (optional)

What you'll do:

  1. Soak mushrooms in hot water until tender, changing water if necessary.
  2. Drain but reserve liquid.
  3. Trim mushrooms and cut into pieces.
  4. Put the oil in a wok or large, deep skillet over medium-high heat.
  5. When hot, add broccoli and mushrooms, and raise heat to high.
  6. Stir to cook until broccoli becomes bright green and glossy and begins to brown, about 5 minutes.
  7. Add salt to taste, the sugar, and the stock. Add the garlic and ginger here, too.
  8. Stir for 15 seconds, then add the mushroom soaking liquid.
  9. Stir and cook until almost all liquid evaporates and broccoli is tender, about 5 minutes.
  10. Stir in soy sauce, taste and adjust the seasoning, and serve.

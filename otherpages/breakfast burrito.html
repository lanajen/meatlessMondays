Breakfast Burritos

For the Monday mornings (or any morning) when you crave a hearty breakfast! You can also multiply the recipe and freeze burritos for the coming days - simply wrap in foil and bake for about 20 minutes at 350 degrees F to reheat.

What you'll need:

  • For basic burrito:
    • 2 cups refried or cooked/drained black or pinto beans
    • 4 large flour tortillas
    • 6 eggs
    • 2 tablespoons butter or extra virgin olive oil
    • 1 cup grated cheddar or Jack cheese
  • Extras, to spruce up your burrito:
    • chopped fresh tomatoes
    • black olives
    • scallions
    • minced fresh chiles
    • avocado
    • small cubes of cooked/pan-fried potatoes
  • Optional sauces for your burrito:
    • Tomato salsa
    • Corn salsa
    • Fruit salsa
    • Pesto
    • Bottled hot sauce
    • Mexican crema

What you'll do:

  1. Warm the beans. To warm the tortillas, wrap in foil and put in a 300 degree F oven for 10 minutes. If you want to go the microwave route, stack tortillas between two damp paper towels and microwave for 30 to 60 seconds.
  2. Beat eggs lightly. Combine with oil (and milk, if you like) on a large skillet over medium-high heat. Stir frequently to cook. If you notice some parts drying out, remove pan from heat and continue stirring until cooking slows down, then return pan to heat and continue cooking.
  3. When eggs are nearly done, remove from heat and assemble the burritos. Spread the cheese on each tortilla and top with about 1/2 cup of beans.
  4. Add the eggs and any sauces or fixings.
  5. Roll up and serve.
